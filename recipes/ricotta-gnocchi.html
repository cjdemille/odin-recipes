Ricotta Gnocchi

Description

Lighter than their potato based cousins, the ricotta gnocci are great for simple preparations like browned butter and sage.

Ingredients

  • Ricotta Cheese
  • Eggs
  • Finely grated parmesan cheese
  • All purpose flour
  • Salt
  • Freshly cracked black pepper

Steps

  1. Combine ricotta and parmesan cheese in a large mixing bowl
  2. Add the eggs and mix well
  3. Season with salt and pepper
  4. Add a few tablespoons of flour to bring the dough together. Be careful not to add too much flour or to overwork the dough.
  5. Allow the dough to chill for at least an hour
  6. On a lightly floured work surface, spoon out a quarter of the dough.
  7. Gently dust with more all purpose flour.
  8. Roll the dough into a 1/2" log
  9. Use a floured knife or bench knife to cut the dough into 1/2" pieces and move to a floured baking sheet.
  10. Repeat with the other 3/4 of the dough
  11. chill the formed gnocchi for at least an hour (up to one day) before cooking
  12. Bring a pot of water to a low boil and add salt
  13. Add the gnocchi to the water and let boil gently for about 5 mintues. When the gnocchi rise to the top and have a sheen, they're ready to come out of the water.
